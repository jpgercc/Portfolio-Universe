The Eternal Husband

While wandering through St. Petersburg, Veltchaninov becomes increasingly disturbed by the persistent presence of a mourning man, without fully understanding why. One day, he could swear the man reached out to him and said his name before disappearing into the crowd. That night, the mysterious figure appears at his door: it is Pavel Pavlovitch, the widower of Natalia Vassilievna, Veltchaninov's former lover. This encounter triggers an emotional and intellectual battle filled with uncertainty, leading the story down unexpected paths.

Spoiler: Some characters are more than one.

I, Robot

"I, Robot" is part of one of Asimov's three major series: Robots, Foundation, and Empire. The book centers on Dr. Susan Calvin, a leading robopsychologist, who recounts stories summarizing the evolution of robotics. Through imaginative and humorous narratives, Asimov introduces readers to the history and dilemmas of robotics. The stories feature robots who go insane, make jokes, read minds, and even outsmart humans. The book also presents the famous Three Laws of Robotics, a cornerstone of science fiction, which lead to complex conflicts that drive the plot.

Ham on Rye

What could be worse than growing up in post-1929 Depression-era America? Being poor, of German descent, covered in acne, having an authoritarian borderline-psychopathic father, a passive and ignorant mother, no girlfriend, and facing the bleak prospect of becoming cheap labor in an increasingly hostile world for sensitive, troubled people. This is the story of Henry Chinaski, the protagonist of one of Charles Bukowski's most moving and widely read novels. A coming-of-age novel with strong autobiographical elements, "Ham on Rye" captivates readers with its brutal honesty and deceptively simple narrative. It reflects the struggle for dignity, truth, and freedom, making it one of the finest American novels of the late 20th century.

Illusions: The Adventures of a Reluctant Messiah

"Illusions: The Adventures of a Reluctant Messiah", by Richard Bach, is a novel that explores the nature of reality and the quest for meaning. The story follows the author, a disillusioned pilot, who meets Donald Shimoda, a former mechanic and a "reluctant messiah". Shimoda possesses the ability to perform small miracles and challenges the protagonist, and the reader, to question limiting beliefs, suggesting that reality is an illusion created for learning and growth.

The Art of War

"The Art of War" by Sun Tzu is an ancient Chinese military treatise that offers deep insights into strategy, leadership, and conflict management. Written over 2,500 years ago, it teaches how to overcome challenges not only in military contexts but also in business, leadership, and life. Sun Tzu emphasizes the importance of knowing yourself and your opponent, planning carefully, and adapting to changing circumstances.

The Prophet

"The Prophet" by Khalil Gibran is a classic book offering profound insights into life through the teachings of a prophet named Almustafa, who is about to leave the city of Orphalese after twelve years of exile. As he prepares to depart, the townspeople ask him to share his wisdom, resulting in 26 poetic essays on love, marriage, children, work, joy, sorrow, and more. The book explores universal themes, inviting readers to reflect deeply on their own lives and find meaning in everyday experiences.
