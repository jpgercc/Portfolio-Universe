Excerpt from the poem "Alone"

From childhood's hour I have not been

As others were—I have not seen

As others saw—I could not bring

My passions from a common spring—

From the same source I have not taken

My sorrow—I could not awaken

My heart to joy at the same tone—

And all I loved—I loved alone—

Edgar Allan Poe

Life

The necessity of life is to die.

The emptiness.

The desires.

Eyes that look want to be looked at.

They desire, and desire to exist.

Life. Death.

To whom do they belong?

This hiatus.

Poets or mathematicians...

Never managed to explain.

The paradox.

The race.

The stagnation.

Bodies fall

like stones.

But...

who is to judge a stone

for falling with the wind?

Where does it come from?

Where does it go?

The wind.

Always returns...

And now,

the wisest of words

is simply;

silence.
